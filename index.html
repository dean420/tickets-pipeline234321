THE HARD RULEWe do not hold a strategy meeting without a completed Discovery Questionnaire. The single exception: partnership conversations (resellers, white-label, strategic alliances) — those skip the questionnaire and go straight to leadership. Everything else: no questionnaire, no meeting.

The Questionnaire Sequence — how to actually get it completed

You may book the meeting before the questionnaire is done — booking creates commitment. But confirming the meeting triggers the reminder sequence, and the meeting only happens if the questionnaire is in.

WhenActionMessage angle
First contactSend the questionnaire link with the meeting invite"5 minutes, 11 questions — it makes our call about you, not about us."
On bookingMeeting goes in the calendar — questionnaire status logged as PENDING in Monday.com"Great — locked in. One thing before we meet…"
On confirmationAutomated + personal reminder to complete the questionnaire"To make our time worth it, please complete this before the call."
48 hrs beforeCheck status. Not done? Call or message personally"Want to make sure our call is tailored to you — 5 minutes is all it takes."
24 hrs beforeStill not done? Offer to complete it together on a 5-min pre-call, or reschedule"Happy to run through it with you in 5 minutes today — or we can move the call."

OUTREACH OPERATING STANDARDS
The daily disciplines behind Stages 1–2. For BDRs and Appointment Setters.

One System of Record

Alta is the engine. Monday.com is the record. Engagement statuses (meeting booked, interested, not interested) get updated in Alta as you work — and mirrored into Monday.com the same day. If the two ever disagree, Monday.com wins. The shared calling list is a working tool for follow-ups; it feeds Monday.com, it never replaces it.

Calling & Campaign Discipline

  • Respect the prospect's time zone on every outbound call — check before dialing, not after.
  • Campaigns run under both LeadStrong.ai and HireAway. Position the right brand for the conversation, and tag which brand on the lead in Monday.com.
  • Stop-on-Reply applies to every channel: the moment a reply lands, automation stops and a live rep takes over.

Appointment Confirmation — a live touch, not just a calendar invite

  • Check the client's calendar availability before proposing times. Coordinate internal schedules (Dean, Cherryl, leadership) before locking anything in.
  • Call the prospect to confirm the appointment. Follow up and reschedule by phone where needed — a confirmed meeting is one a human has confirmed.
  • The booking-confirmation email re-attaches the questionnaire link — but the questionnaire is sent at FIRST CONTACT, never as a post-meeting courtesy. The meeting only happens if it's completed (Stage 2 hard rule).

Reporting Rhythm

  • End of every day: send the engagement report — calls made, emails sent, replies, meetings booked.
  • Every week, before the Monday pipeline review: update the sales dashboard with current outreach and pipeline metrics. The Monday review runs off this dashboard — if it isn't updated, the review is blind.

🟢 POSITIVE BUYING SIGNALS — lean in
  • "How fast could we start?" — timeline questions
  • "Who would actually work on my account?" — team questions
  • "What does the first month look like?" — process detail
  • They ask you to repeat or re-show pricing
  • They bring in a colleague or say "my partner should hear this"
  • They start talking as if it's happening: "when we launch…"
  • Contract questions: term length, what happens after 90 days
🔴 CAUTION SIGNALS — slow down, ask why
  • Silence after pricing — don't talk past it; ask what they're thinking
  • "Send me something and I'll look at it" — vague deferral
  • Repeatedly comparing us to a cheaper DIY option
  • Can't articulate who their ideal customer is
  • No authority — the real decision-maker isn't on the call
  • "We tried something like this and it didn't work" — unpack it before moving on
